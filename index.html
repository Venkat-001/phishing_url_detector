How to Handle Phishing URLs

If you identify a phishing URL, the most important actions are to avoid interacting with it, report it to authorities, and take protective measures if you already clicked it.

1. If You Have NOT Clicked the Link

  • Do not click or interact with the link.
  • Delete the message immediately.
  • Report to NCSC.GOV.UK

2. If You DID Click the Link

  • Close the page immediately.
  • Disconnect from the internet.
  • Run a full antivirus scan.
  • Change passwords for affected accounts.
  • Clear browser cache and cookies.

3. How to Report

  • Forward suspicious emails to report@phishing.gov.uk
  • Forward suspicious texts to 7726.
  • Report to Google Safe Browsing.

Signs of a Phishing Link

  • Mismatched domains (e.g., micros0ft.com).
  • URL shorteners hiding the true destination.
  • Urgent language: "Your account will be suspended".

Phishing Awareness

Phishing is a cyber attack where attackers trick you into revealing sensitive information.

  • Check the URL carefully
  • Never share passwords or OTPs
  • Enable 2FA on important accounts
  • Look for HTTPS
  • Verify sender identity

Why Phishing Matters

Phishing attacks cost individuals and companies millions every year.

Definition

Phishing is a scam where attackers pretend to be trusted entities to steal your information.

Statistics

Tips
